2.Newton Raphson Method

1.Start with an initial guess: You begin with an initial estimate (x0) for the root of the function f(x) = 0.

2.Draw the tangent: At the point (x0, f(x0)), draw the tangent line to the curve of f(x).

3.Find the intersection: Determine the point (x1) where this tangent line intersects the x-axis.

4.Iterate: The point x1 becomes the new estimate for the root, and you repeat the process by drawing a tangent at (x1, f(x1)) and finding the new intersection point (x2), and so on.

5.Continue until convergence: Repeat steps 2-4 until the successive approximations (xi) converge to a desired level of accuracy, meaning the difference between successive iterations becomes small.

6.Newton-Raphson formula: The core of the method lies in the formula:
Code

x_(n+1) = x_n - f(x_n) / f'(x_n)
where:
x_(n+1) is the next approximation of the root.
x_n is the current approximation of the root.
f(x_n) is the value of the function at x_n.
f'(x_n) is the derivative of the function at x_n.
In essence, the Newton-Raphson method approximates the root of a function by repeatedly drawing tangent lines and finding their x-intercepts, iteratively refining the approximation until it converges to a solution.
